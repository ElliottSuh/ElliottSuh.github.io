This world lay waste in white — frozen yet decaying in this hell called Eden. Truly ironic of name. A paradise that was never meant to be, yet here we are. Forced to fix a problem that could have been avoided.

I still think to myself: Would it have been better if I hadn’t taken this request?

The wind howling across the ruins of what used to be Sector 7-Delta, a commercial district that once housed over fifty thousand souls. Now, what remains is just twisted metal and ice-encrusted concrete, a monument to the Collapse that claimed this world. Thirty-seven days ago, the Neo-Thermal Generators went offline. Thirty-six days ago, the eternal winter began.

We got shipped a month ago to provide security and evacuation protocols — but the evac ship we were supposed to get on got blown to bits, courtesy of the damn scavengers. So now… my division wanders around this vast frozen —

An Energetic Voice "Hey, are you still doing a monologue?"

I glance up from where I'm sitting, balanced on a chunk of collapsed overpass. It's Jake — the youngest in our division — grinning under his thermal mask, breath misting in the frigid air. He's been trying to strike up a conversation with me since we got here. I don't know if it's his attempt to break the ice or just his personality, but either way, he's been... persistent. Eighteen years old and still thinks everything's an adventure. Sometimes I envy that. Other times, I wonder how he's managed to stay alive this long with that attitude.

LEAD [NARRATOR] "Really. You just had to cut me off mid sentence?"
JAKE "Yo, my name's Jake, Jake the Paragon Advocate, but call me Jake."
LEAD "You know, we're supposed to keep our identities a secret since we're on this type of mission."

I don’t know what to do with this kid. At least he listens better than he acts — for kids his age, especially the ones at the AGENCY. Getting up from a pile of crates marked with faded Ulacylon Megagroup logos, I stretch my legs and walk toward the chatting group positioned further along the ruins. Jake bolts past me before I take any step, leaving fresh shoe prints in the snow.

The makeshift camp we’ve established isn’t much — a clearing in the ruins, protected on three sides by collapsed buildings, with a clear sightline to the main thoroughfare. A standard tactical positioning, except nothing about this mission has been standard so far. We’ve been holed up here for six hours, long enough for the team to start getting restless. But if that wasn’t enough, we’ve been stranded on the winter abomination for a month, Month. This job was suppose to be a quick easy in and out job, not freeze, shot, and beat to death mission.

The group was split in two. Over by a crumbling wall, Daniel, Erika, Rose, and Shin are deep in a discussion about off-topic things and drinks — the kind of conversation you only have when you’re trying to forget where you are or if you’re done with the job type conversation.

ERIC "Oh hey, it's boss."
LEAD "I think we've rested enough here. It's about time to head out."
ERIC "Sounds good, boss."

The other cluster — Charlotte, Epoch, Aaron, and Clementine — are having a darker conversation. I catch the tail end of it as I cross over.

EPOCH "I would be anywhere but here, in this nuclear winter wasteland."
AARON "The Collapse. The damned Collapse. If only Ulacylon fucking cared enough, we wouldn't have to clean their shit up, and I wouldn't have to be here."
CHARLOTTE "Come on now. There's no need to cry over spilled milk."
CLEMENTINE "Yet they sent us here with their equipment to clean up their mess."

Aaron wasn't wrong. The Collapse wasn't natural. The Agency's own intelligence pointed toward deliberate sabotage — someone wanted Eden's generators offline. Someone with access, with motive, and with the kind of cold calculation that only a handful of people on this planet possess. The name floating around is VALORII — a Tier 5 agent from Wave 3. Rogue. Unpredictable. Believed to still be on Eden.

Origin: Hedgeline, northeast sector
Count estimate: 10–16 units
Classification: SCAVENGER (armed)
ADDITIONAL SIGNATURE DETECTED — REIKGON-CLASS ENTITY
SHIN "Sorry to cut in — but we've got suspicious movement along the hedgeline."
LEAD "Battle positions — and put on your headsets."

A hail of bullets starts spraying across the street, ricocheting, leaving craters in road and nearby buildings. A stray round catches a gas pump. The explosion lights the grey sky orange for half a second — and then, underneath the ringing, something else. A sound that has nothing to do with gunfire.

A shriek. Long, mournful, and utterly inhuman.

JAKE "Ah, shit. Here we go again. We've got a crying boy."
AARON "These bastards angered a Sickler. As if this fight wasn't enough."
LEAD "I'm deploying Juggernaut Kit — buy me some time."

I place the heavy box down on the ice, fingers moving fast. One eye on the display on my arm — the AGENT Watch. A countdown. A reminder I don't need: sepsis clock ticking, extraction window open, ten days left.

The Arrival

The sound of snow layers crunching beneath my boots. My warm breath fogging in the air, there and gone.

LEAD [NARRATOR] "This cold. Winter actually came."

I stepped into the Agency's east courtyard. The cold breeze swept past my cheek, carrying nothing with it — no sound from the lower districts, no transit hum, just the stillness of early morning and the garden sitting there in front of me like it always did. I let out another slow breath and watched it disappear into the grey morning air.

The garden that shouldn't exist — and that was exactly why I kept coming back. Roses in winter. Grass that stayed green regardless of season. Hedges trimmed to a precision that no wind had disturbed. Trees bearing fruit in dead winter, silent and unbothered. There was something about that stillness — the fabricated order of it — that kept drawing me back out here, standing in the cold, thinking to myself: how does anything grow like this? Not just survive, but thrive, bloom, bear fruit, as if the seasons didn't exist. No one had ever given me a satisfying answer. Agency botanists had theories, atmospheric engineers had their own theories, but none of them held past the first question. Whatever kept this garden alive, New Earth wasn't explaining it — and that silence, somehow, felt more deliberate than the garden itself.

Eventually the garden's silence pressed in close enough that my eyes drifted upward, toward the city instead.

Sovereignty rose behind the garden walls — tier upon tiers of glass and pale stone, the Capital of New Earth climbing toward a sky that had been scrubbed of smog through expensive atmospheric filters and considerable political will. The transit lines threaded between towers. Heating vents pushed white columns of steam into the air. Apartment windows caught the early light, and behind them, people were waking up to another ordinary day of not thinking about what existed beyond the planet's carefully maintained boundaries.

I used to envy that. Twelve years ago, maybe.

This was New Earth — home to many now, after the years of pollution built from innovation and a consistent lack of environmental conscience. That was the soft telling. The harder one went like this: Old Earth didn't deteriorate. It was consumed. The Corpo Wars leveraged every ecosystem as a battlefield asset until there was nothing left to leverage. All previous life, completely terminated. The history books called it a reset.

// New Earth — Context File

Constructed by the Ulacylon Megagroup Corporation following the Corpo Wars. Twice the landmass of any prior continent. Governed by corporate law. Capital city: Sovereignty — a tiered metropolitan structure built vertically, atmospheric filters keeping the sky perpetually clean. New Earth became the de facto home of humanity after Old Earth's termination.

It is not a democracy. It is not a republic. It is a product — maintained, curated, and owned.

Background
Intelligence Briefing
The Frozen World

In the far future, humanity has long since outgrown a single world. Old Earth died of its own making — wars and pollution terminated every living thing across its surface. In its place, the Ulacylon Megagroup Corporation rebuilt a new planet from scratch: New Earth, twice the size of any continent that came before, governed entirely by corporate law.

But New Earth was never the final frontier. For intergalactic expansion, humanity needed a midpoint between the Solar System and deeper space — and they found it in a distant system orbiting a dying white dwarf star designated 227. On one of its planets, they built a civilization. They called the planet Eden.

DESIGNATION: Eden-227
STAR CLASS: White Dwarf (System-227)
STELLAR HEAT OUTPUT: INSUFFICIENT FOR HABITATION
NEO-THERMAL GENERATORS: Previously deployed planet-wide
CURRENT GENERATOR STATUS: OFFLINE — CAUSE: THE COLLAPSE
SURFACE TEMPERATURE: −28°C (DROPPING)
Currency: Odens / Bonds (non-transferable off-world)
Government Status: Single planetary nation — collapsed

The Collapse. Someone deliberately sabotaged Eden's Neo-Thermal Generator network. Without them, the planet plunged into a permanent winter. Eternal cold. Endless storm. The Agency's third wave of Agents — led by the formidable Valorii and the rogue-turned-scavenger-king Yosef — conducted emergency evacuations. Most civilians were pulled off-planet. Most.

// SYSTEMIC INFECTION — Population Average Eden-227
Percentage 66% — Moderate. Decontamination and Liquidation protocols recommended.

What remained was a lawless, ice-choked world full of scavengers, organized raiders, shadow-cults, and something older and darker that had been festering on Eden long before the lights went out. The Eden Restoration Unit (ERU), a private military company, moved in to "restore order" — which in practice meant shooting anyone who moved.

Into this hell, the Agency sent the second wave. That was us.

Shadows
Entity Dossier // Classified
Reikgon's Shadows
// REIKGON — Entity Overview

An entity of unknown nature and origin. Not confirmed human. Not confirmed supernatural. Known objective: the total demise of everything on Eden. Creates "Shadows" — abominations fused with a live human host — to carry out its will. The host dies when the Shadow dies. Whether the hosts were once civilians, scavengers, or willing participants remains unknown. We could be killing innocent people. We don't get the luxury of hesitating about it.

THE SICKLER
Extremely aggressive, but in its docile state — deeply, audibly sad. It wanders slowly, sobbing to itself. Do not approach. Do not make loud noise near it. The moment it detects disturbance, its restraining chains break off and it enters a full rage state, pursuing every living thing on the map with inhuman speed and health. The Sickler has a sadistic urge to mutilate — slashing throats, dealing catastrophic damage with relentless intensity. The scavengers who triggered it outside our position learned this the hard way.
THE SKINNER
A psychologically sophisticated predator. It hides within a human host and mimics a weak, vulnerable scavenger — deliberately appearing as easy prey to lure stronger targets close. When a victim is within striking range, it reveals its true form with an ear-splitting scream designed to shock before it attacks with a massive spiked limb. The screaming-before-killing is tactical. It is not mindless. Treat any lone, disheveled-looking figure with extreme caution.
THE HANGER
Psychological warfare given form. Short exposure causes thoughts of giving up; extended exposure can be lethal in mentally fragile individuals. Its key attack forces the victim's own hand to act against them — a psychic compulsion that can be fought off with significant effort. Notably: Hanger reserves particular hatred for Apostle Agents, calling them "traitors" and abandoning its usual methods to attack physically instead. Agents classified as "Damned" appear to be fully immune to the Hanger's psychological effects.
THE HIDDEN
Invisible. Whispers from the dark as a method of psychological torment before closing in to slash the throat. It turns briefly visible during the moment of attack — the only window to retaliate. Weak by Shadow standards, but its invisibility makes every engagement a gamble. If you are hearing whispers and no one is speaking — do not move. Wait for the flash of visible form. Do not swing blindly.
